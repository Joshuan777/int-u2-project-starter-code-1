What is Poverty?

Poverty are adults and children that have no place of shelter, lack of food, education, water, and health services.

It's a state where people lack resources and financial income struggling to live within their society.

There's not just one main cause that can lead someone into poverty, but multiple causes that can have a person in poverty.

The Impact Poverty has Done

Around the world, there's estimate amount of 700 million people that are poor and are in poverty. That's 8.5% of the global population, surviving on less than $2.15 a day. With the Covid-19 pandemic, economic disruptions, job losses, and rising living causes more families into deeper poverty or go into poverty.

How people get into poverty are for many different reasons. Like the conflict, epidemics like Covid-19, and natural disasters. Furthermore, there can be lack of resources, healthcare, clean water, education, social support, infrastructure and personal safety nets. There are many more problems that led people into poverty and is still a growing problem around the world.
